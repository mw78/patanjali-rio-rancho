Yoga is Samadhi

"Yoga Chittavritti Nirodha"

Maharshi Patanjali, ancient rishi and founder of the Yoga Sutras (ca. 250 B.C.) defines Yog as concentration.

The above saying translates to "stoppage of the fluctuations of the mind."

Sequence

1. Conditioning

ૐ

Conditioning is a method that helps to increase focus and concentration. It is often done guided with the instructor or guru reciting a prayer.

2. Asanas

ૐ

The Asanas I am certified to teach are taken from traditional Hatha Yoga. They are chosen based on the overall directional stretch of the spine and are beginner friendly. They can be easily adjusted to match more difficult variations.

3. Pranayamas

ૐ

Pranayams or Pranayamas are breathing exercises to increase the capacity of your lungs and help with oxygen and blood flow. Depending on individual conditions there are special varieties that can aid in overall balance of the system. There are also Pranayams that are safe for everyone to practice, i.e. Brahmari.

4. Meditation

ૐ

Every Yoga session should finish with mindful relaxation. This is often achieved through Shavasana ("The Corpse Pose"). Similar to conditioning at the beginning of each session, you can finish with a prayer or simply by chanting Aum.

About Me

My love for Yoga did not develop overnight, it took me a few years to get into it. Being someone who used to enjoy running a lot, I found it rather difficult to sit down on a mat and do Asanas. However, after giving it another try, I was astounded by the results that I got from practicing Kundalini Yoga. Kundalini Yoga opened up the door to meditation and my love for Indian culture and philosophy. I wanted to learn more about Yogic Philosophy and traditional forms of Yoga and I wanted to learn from Indian teachers.


After being in lockdown for such a long period, it gave me the opportunity to look inward but also do some research. The Yoga Institute Santacruz, Mumbai is the oldest accredited Yoga school in the world. The current guru, Dr. Hansaji Yogendra, wife of the late Dr. Yogendra, had an influential role in the formulation of a formal syllabus for Yoga schools across the world. To this day she is one of India's most influential experts in the field of Yoga. I thoroughly enjoyed my learning experience with my Indian teachers and "batch mates". I miss them dearly. We still message each other or meet up via Zoom to practice together.


The sequence that I follow is the sequence that I learned from my Indian teachers. I was also trained in Kriyas and Pranayamas which I practice. Although I practice Kundalini yoga, I do not teach it. The asanas that I am certified to teach are traditional and safe beginners friendly asanas taken from Hatha Yoga. If you are interested in learning about Yogic Philosophy and techniques that go beyond obscure asanas performed by Western celebrities, I suggest you check out their website.


Where I got certified as RYT 200: The Yoga Institute Santacruz, Mumbai.
